Once you can read at a low-intermediate level, switching to a monolingual
dictionary is one of the biggest jumps in fluency you can make. Baidu Hanyu
(百度汉语) is what native speakers actually use: definitions written in
clear Chinese, full 成语 (idiom) source-texts with translations, classical
poetry attached to common characters, and example sentences pulled from
real writing. Interface is Chinese-only, which is the point — sitting with
it forces you to read your way into the language instead of around it.
